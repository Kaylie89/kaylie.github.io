Understanding Industry 4.0:
Industry 4.0 refers to the integration of advanced digital technologies into traditional business processes, creating smart factories and interconnected systems. Examples include:

  • Smart Manufacturing: Utilizing IoT (Internet of Things) to monitor and optimize production processes in real-time.
  • Predictive Maintenance: Using sensors and data analytics to predict equipment failures before they occur, reducing downtime.

Real-World Examples of Risks:

  • Cybersecurity Threats: Increased connectivity can lead to vulnerabilities in cyber-attacks.
  • Data Privacy Concerns: Handling large volumes of data can lead to breaches of sensitive information.

Supporting/Contradicting Article:
A study by Smith et al. (2020) supports the risk categories identified by Kovaitė and Stankevičienė, emphasizing the importance of robust cybersecurity measures in Industry 4.0 environments.
